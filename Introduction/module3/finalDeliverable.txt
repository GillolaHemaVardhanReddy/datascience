Final deliverables refer to the completed outputs or products that result from a project or process, 
which are presented to stakeholders, clients, or evaluators. These deliverables are intended to 
demonstrate the outcomes, findings, or conclusions of the work undertaken.

1. practical context : Final deliverables in practical settings are typically the communicated insights 
derived from analytics. These insights can be used by stakeholders to make informed decisions or formulate 
strategies and policies.

2. academic context : In academia, final deliverables often take the form of essays or reports. 
These documents comprehensively present the findings of research or analysis, typically ranging from 
1,000 to 7,000 words in length. They include a detailed narrative that explains the data, the analysis 
performed, the insights gained, and the implications of those insights.
